MISSION

To protect and promote the right of every Filipino to quality, equitable, culture-based, and complete basic education where:

Students learn in a child-friendly, gender sensitive, safe and motivating environment

Teachers facilitate learning and constantly nurture every learner

Administrators and staff, as stewards of the institution, ensure an enabling and supportive environment for effective learning to happen

Family, community and other stakeholders are actively engaged and share responsibility for developing lifelong learners

Strand's

STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics)

The STEM strand focuses on science, mathematics, and technology-related subjects. It is designed for students who want to pursue careers in engineering, medicine, computer science, and other technical fields. This strand enhances problem-solving, critical thinking, and analytical skills, preparing students for college courses that require strong scientific and mathematical foundations.

ABM (Accountancy, Business, and Management)

The ABM strand is ideal for students who are interested in business, finance, marketing, and entrepreneurship. It provides knowledge in economics, financial management, business ethics, and marketing strategies. This strand prepares students for business-related courses in college and equips them with skills to start and manage their own businesses.

HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences)

The HUMSS strand focuses on social sciences, communication, and critical thinking. It is best for students who want to pursue careers in law, journalism, education, psychology, and public service. This strand enhances skills in research, writing, and public speaking, preparing students for courses that involve social interaction and analysis.

GAS (General Academic Strand)

The GAS strand is designed for students who are undecided about their career paths. It offers a mix of subjects from STEM, ABM, and HUMSS, providing a balanced education in different disciplines. This strand is suitable for those who want to explore various fields before choosing a specific college course.

TVL (Technical-Vocational-Livelihood) Track

The TVL track focuses on practical skills and hands-on training in various fields, such as agriculture, information and communication technology (ICT), home economics, and industrial work. This track prepares students for immediate employment after graduation by offering certifications that qualify them for skilled jobs. It is best for students who prefer technical work over academic subjects.

Facilities

Computer Labolarator

A computer laboratory is a dedicated learning space equipped with computers, internet access, and essential software to support digital learning. In this lab, students gain hands-on experience in programming, networking, and hardware troubleshooting. They use it for coding, simulations, and scientific research, as well as for business analytics, accounting software, and marketing presentations.

Home Economics workshop

A Home Economics workshop is a hands-on learning space equipped with kitchen tools, sewing machines, and other household essentials designed to teach practical life skills. It is mainly used by students in the TVL (Technical-Vocational-Livelihood) Track, particularly those specializing in Home Economics. In this workshop, students learn various skills such as cooking, baking, sewing, housekeeping, and food service management.

Dress-making Workshop

A dressmaking workshop is a specialized learning space equipped with sewing machines, fabric materials, measuring tools, and other essential equipment for creating garments. In Senior High School, this workshop is primarily used by students under the TVL (Technical-Vocational-Livelihood) Track, specifically in the Fashion and Dressmaking strand. Here, students learn essential skills such as fabric cutting, sewing techniques, pattern-making, and garment construction.
